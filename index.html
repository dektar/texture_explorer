Texture mapping explorer

Katie Dektar / CS248A final project 2024

Interactively visualize how a 2D texture map can be wrapped over geometry and projected into screen space.

Warp speed

Below are two images. The first is is a 3D rendering of a textured model, and the second is a 2D visualization of texture space. You can see how the texture is warped in the first compared to the second during the transformations that take it from object space to screen space.

Draw your own!

Click or tap and drag to draw on the 2D or 3D model see your drawings reflected into both the texture and the textured geometry. Note how different your drawings look in 2D or 3D! If you draw a recognizable shape on one side it may get all warped on the other side. Challenge: Can you draw a shape on the flat texture (second image) that looks square on the 3D plane (first)? To start over, clear your drawing with the controls below.

Use the controls to modify position, size, or type of object, the texture mapping interpolation method, or to reset the texture map to different defaults. Try it: Change the model to the cube. Can you figure out which parts of the texture are mapped to which cube face?
